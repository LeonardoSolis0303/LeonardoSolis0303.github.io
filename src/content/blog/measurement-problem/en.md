I have been thinking for weeks about why wave function collapse remains
so conceptually uncomfortable. It is not that there are no technical
answers — there are several. None of them satisfies me completely.

## The standard answers

The Copenhagen interpretation says: the wave function collapses upon
measurement. What does *measurement* mean? It does not say. Many-worlds
says: no collapse, everything happens. Why do we see only one outcome?
Not clearly explained either.

Decoherence does a beautiful job explaining how the appearance of
classicality emerges, but it does not, by itself, solve the problem of
selection — why this outcome rather than that one.

## My current position

The problem is not technical, it is conceptual. We need a better
understanding of what *information* means in the quantum context before
we can give a satisfying answer to the measurement problem.

I am suspicious of any framing that takes the wave function as
ontologically primary. The wave function is a *prediction*, an organized
expectation about future measurements. Treating it as a thing leads to
puzzles like collapse. Treating it as a representation does not — but
then we owe an explanation of *what* the underlying thing is.
